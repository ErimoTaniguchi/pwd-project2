Wakayama

Sacred Mountains and Coastal Beauty

Back to Destinations

Overview

Wakayama Prefecture is a hidden gem of Japan, famous for the sacred Kumano Kodo pilgrimage trails and the spiritual Mount Koya. This region offers a perfect blend of mountain spirituality, coastal beauty, and traditional Japanese culture. Experience ancient temples, pristine beaches, and some of Japan's finest hot springs.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November)

Recommended Duration

3-5 days recommended

Highlights

Kumano Kodo pilgrimage trails

Mount Koya (Koyasan) temples and monasteries

Nachi Falls - Japan's tallest waterfall

Major Attractions

Kumano Kodo

Ancient pilgrimage routes through the sacred Kumano mountains. Walk in the footsteps of emperors and samurai on these UNESCO World Heritage trails surrounded by pristine forests.

Mount Koya (Koyasan)

A sacred mountain town and the center of Shingon Buddhism, home to over 100 temples. Stay overnight in a temple lodging (shukubo) and experience Buddhist vegetarian cuisine and morning prayers.

Nachi Taisha Shrine

A stunning vermillion shrine set against the backdrop of Nachi Falls, Japan's tallest waterfall at 133 meters. One of the three Grand Shrines of Kumano.

Travel Tips

  • Book temple lodgings (shukubo) in advance, especially during peak seasons
  • Wear comfortable hiking shoes for the Kumano Kodo trails
  • Try the local specialty: Wakayama ramen with tonkotsu soy sauce broth
