Kagoshima

The Naples of the Eastern World

Back to Destinations

Overview

Known as the 'Naples of the Eastern World' for its bay and active volcano, Kagoshima offers a unique blend of natural beauty, samurai history, and subtropical culture. Dominated by the smoking Sakurajima volcano, this southern city is famous for its black pork (kurobuta), shochu spirits, and warm hospitality. The region's mild climate supports lush vegetation and beautiful gardens.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November)

Recommended Duration

2-4 days recommended

Highlights

Sakurajima - active volcano accessible by ferry

Samurai district and historical sites

Sengan-en Garden with volcano viewsl

Major Attractions

Sakurajima Volcano

One of Japan's most active volcanoes, constantly smoking and occasionally erupting. Take a 15-minute ferry ride from Kagoshima city and explore the volcanic landscape, visit hot spring foot baths, and learn about volcanic activity at the visitor center.

Sengan-en Garden

A stunning feudal lord's villa and garden designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The garden incorporates Sakurajima volcano and Kinko Bay into its design - a technique called 'borrowed scenery.' Includes historical buildings and a museum.

Ibusuki Sand Baths

A unique experience where you're buried in naturally heated volcanic sand on the beach. The therapeutic heat is said to improve circulation and relieve muscle pain. Located about an hour south of Kagoshima city.

Travel Tips

  • Carry an umbrella for volcanic ash from Sakurajima
  • Sample Kagoshima's shochu, a traditional Japanese spirit
  • Don't miss the Kagoshima ramen with its rich pork broth
