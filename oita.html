Oita

The Hot Spring Capital of Japan

Back to Destinations

Overview

Oita Prefecture boasts more hot spring sources and the highest volume of hot spring water in all of Japan. Known as the 'Onsen Prefecture,' it offers unique bathing experiences from sand baths to mud baths, along with stunning natural landscapes, historic castles, and delicious local cuisine including fugu (pufferfish) and Bungo beef.

Best Time to Visit

Year-round; Winter (December-February) ideal for hot springs

Recommended Duration

3-4 days recommended

Highlights

Beppu - one of Japan's most famous hot spring towns

Yufuin - charming onsen village with mountain views

Jigoku Meguri (Hell Tour) - colorful hot spring pools

Major Attractions

Beppu Hells (Jigoku)

Eight spectacular hot springs for viewing rather than bathing. Each 'hell' has unique features - from cobalt blue pools to blood-red water and erupting mud. The most iconic is Umi Jigoku (Sea Hell) with its stunning blue color.

Yufuin

A picturesque hot spring town nestled at the foot of Mount Yufu. Stroll along Lake Kinrinko, browse art galleries and boutiques, and soak in outdoor baths with mountain views. Less crowded than Beppu.

Usuki Stone Buddhas/h3>

Over 60 stone Buddhist statues carved into cliffs in the 12th century. These nationally designated treasures showcase remarkable craftsmanship and are set in a peaceful rural landscape.

Travel Tips

  • Bring a small towel - many onsens don't provide them
  • Try the unique sand bath (sunamushi) experience in Beppu
  • Sample the local specialty: toriten (chicken tempura)
